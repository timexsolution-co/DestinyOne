# DestinyOne Privacy Policy — Launch Draft

**Status:** Product draft for legal review. Replace bracketed fields and publish at a public HTTPS URL before store submission.

**Effective date:** [DATE]

DestinyOne helps adults discover serious relationships and marriage-minded connections. This policy explains how [LEGAL COMPANY NAME] collects, uses, and protects information when you use DestinyOne.

## Information we collect

- Account details such as phone number, email address, and authentication records
- Profile details such as age, city, profession, photos, relationship intent, values, and optional community or religion
- Verification submissions such as a private selfie and, if offered, an optional identity document
- Content you choose to send, including messages, photos, voice introductions, gifts, reports, and date proposals
- In-app activity such as profiles viewed, skipped, or liked, used to improve recommendations
- Approximate foreground location only when you enable Crossed Paths or request nearby date ideas
- Subscription and transaction status from Apple or Google; full payment-card details are not collected by DestinyOne
- Device, diagnostic, security, and fraud-prevention information

DestinyOne does not read browser history, unrelated phone searches, private contacts, or activity in other apps to choose matches.

## How we use information

We use information to operate authentication, verification, matching, messaging, subscriptions, safety tools, customer support, fraud prevention, product analytics, and legal compliance. Matching uses relationship intent, values, lifestyle signals, and in-app interactions. Members see qualitative labels rather than compatibility percentages.

## Sharing

We share information only with service providers needed to operate the app, other members according to profile and messaging controls, authorities when legally required, or a successor in a properly managed business transaction. We do not sell personal information. Final legal review must confirm disclosures required by applicable US state privacy laws.

## Location

Location features are opt-in. The product should store and display an approximate area—not a member’s exact trail. Crossed Paths requires mutual eligibility and delayed display. Background location is not required.

## Retention and deletion

Information is kept only as long as needed for the purposes above, safety, dispute handling, and legal obligations. Members can request account deletion in the app. Production operations must define deletion timelines, backup expiry, and any narrow safety-retention exceptions.

## Safety and age

DestinyOne is for adults age 18 and older. The intended launch audience is ages 25–35. Reports, blocks, and safety records may be reviewed to enforce community rules and protect members.

## Your choices

Depending on location, members may request access, correction, deletion, portability, or restriction of certain processing. Members can disable Smart Discovery signals, Crossed Paths, camera, microphone, photos, and location through the app or device settings.

## Security and international processing

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards. No system is perfectly secure. If information is processed outside a member’s state or country, appropriate contractual and legal safeguards will be used.
