# DestinyOne network-effect strategy

Dating apps win city-by-city. DestinyOne should avoid opening everywhere at once; the product should build dense, verified communities in a few high-value markets first.

## Launch city order

1. **NYC** — New York / Jersey City
2. **Bay Area** — San Francisco / San Jose
3. **Dallas** — Dallas / Plano / Frisco
4. **Toronto** — Toronto / Brampton / Mississauga
5. **Chicago** — Chicago / Naperville / Schaumburg

Each city should target roughly **180–250 verified singles** before broad launch, with a larger waitlist behind it.

## Growth loops now modeled in the app

- City-by-city density launch plan
- Waitlist / invite system
- Referral rewards
- Friend vouch loop
- Community ambassadors
- Indian professional events
- College, alumni, and professional groups
- Success stories
- “Bring 3 verified friends” challenge

## Production connection points

- Waitlist table: city, invite source, profession, community group, verification status.
- Referral rewards: reward only after invited members verify and remain in good standing.
- Ambassadors: invite codes, hosted events, safety score, attendance quality.
- Events: city calendars for Indian professional mixers, alumni nights, founder dinners, video speed dates.
- Success stories: collect consent, city, timeline, safety notes, and public/private story approval.

## Safety and brand boundary

Network growth should not become spam. Reward quality verified invites, friend vouches, and community-hosted events — not random mass invites.
